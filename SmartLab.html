How can I help scientists to improve work efficiency

As a scientist by myself, I watched many researchers having the same trouble on improving their work efficiency.
The nature of work requires them to perform multiple tasks with different timelines, equipments and locations in parallel. Meanwhile, they have to memorize all the checkpoints and document any observations and results they got during the experiments.

Take-away: A schedule management system may help to increase lab workers' efficiency.
User research results summary

Possible solutions based on user needs

Think of opportunities in term of different challenges in a scientific lab environment:


  • Share schedule with others in a sharing workspace;
  • Optimize work schedule to minimize fragmented time within each experiment;
  • Document experimental details to maintain repeatable results;
  • Arrange work order to avoid energy wasting on traveling between multiple operational locations.

Researvation system is a promising solution

After analyzing a LAB workspace map, I found it can be divided into several blocks based on its functionality. Although various problems may happen in each block, designing a reservation system becomes a common solution for addressing problems across different blocks.

LAB workspace analysis

Final concept

Combining the features of reservation, path calculation and schedule management, I designed a system being able to optimize a customized working plan based on usersâ€™ needs.

  • Meeting Room Reservation System (Cisco): Avoid conflicts of work space or equipments;
  • Notes & Reminder (Google Calendar): Keep experimental details;
  • Time Management (Mapquest app): Utilize waiting time during each experiment;
  • Path Finder (Amazon Robotics): Save energy by decreasing traveling.
