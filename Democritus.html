Democritus is a science editorial website aimed at engaging more people in learning basic science and having a better online reading experience.

Type
Individual course project
Deliverables
Prototypes (Desktop & mobile);
Interaction Demos;
Timeline
2019-10-07 - 2019-10-20

People don't reading science content online.

Science becomes boring when you have to read a long paper discussing some unfamiliar knowledgement word by word.

The problem I identified for current science editorial websites is a lack of attraction and motivation for people to engage in reading science content as well as keep revisiting the sites.

User groups
" As a college student, I want to quickly document and organize the information that is useful for me, so that I can take away for my future references.
" As a high school student, I want to find out what a scientist's work looks like, so that I can think about whether I'd like to choose it as my career.
" As a high school teacher, I want to browse interesting science-related materials, so that I can use them as my teaching resources.

How can I keep audiences scrolling.

  • Create curiosity: design a weekly spotlight on controversial issues and disccusion forum;
  • Fill the gap: design a scientists' blog section about their stories and industry insights;
  • Set up challenge: design a science quiz questions section;
  • Build sense of achievement: design a note-taking widget on the post page.
Information Architecture

Decisions on website structure

"Create curiosity" is the priority goal in this project, therefore, weekly spotlight, or to say the discussion forum section should be prioritized to draw users' attention at the first glance.
